Player Bios




Othman Ennaciri

Born and raised in Morocco, Othman Ennaciri grew up in a tennis environment. From grand-father to grandson, the racket was handed to him at a very early age. He started competing at the age of 8 years old and was one of the top players in his country. Othman moved to Canada in 2014 at the age of 17, to pursue a Bachelor specialized in accounting at the University of Ottawa. He graduated in December 2018, and is now 23 years old. His best shot is his forehand.

Fun fact about Othman: At the age of 13, he forgot his shoes at home going to play a quarter final in a national tournament. He made sure that it doesn’t happen again.



Hannah Atkinson

Hannah grew up playing competitive tennis in Toronto. She represented Team Ontario in the Junior Nationals from the age of 12 to 18. During her undergrad, Hannah played on the varsity Women’s Tennis Team at Williams College. She was a member of the 2015 NCAA National Championship Team. As a co-captain in 2017, she lead the team to win the NCAA National Team Championship. Also in 2017, she achieved an All-American status in doubles with partner Chloe Henderson. She received the Intercollegiate Tennis Association (ITA) National Most Improved Senior Award.

Fun Fact: While playing for the University of Ottawa Tennis Club, Hannah remains undefeated in singles and doubles.



Emma McShane

Born and raised in Halifax, Nova Scotia, Emma started playing tennis at the age of four and then went on to compete at the national level. In 2017, she was selected to be the captain of Team Nova Scotia at the Canada Summer Games in Winnipeg, Manitoba where she represented her province alongside her seven other teammates.

Tennis has always been an integral part of Emma’s life and she is very fortunate to be able to keep playing, even while pursuing her studies. Emma is currently an undergraduate student in Human Kinetics at the University of Ottawa.




Andrew Clark-Alfaro

Andrew Clark-Alfaro is a nineteen-year-old going into his third year at the University of Ottawa. He is in the Bachelor of Commerce/Juris Doctor program and will be starting law school in September 2020. He started playing tennis regularly when he was 12 and his best shot is his serve.

A fun fact about him is he eats way too much pita bread and hummus.



Miteau Butskhrikidze

Miteau is starting his fourth year in his combined Political Science and Juris Doctor program at the University of Ottawa this fall. Nineteen years old, he has been playing competitive tennis since the age of ten and coaching tennis for the last five years at the Wychwood Tennis Club. His best shot is probably his serve (when it goes in), but his favorite are volleys.

Fun fact: his good footwork in tennis might come from one of his other favorite hobbies: step dancing.



Mahmoud Shedid

Introducing Mahmoud Shedid, also known as Dada is an Egyptian 2nd year student at the University of Ottawa studying Finance at the Telfer school of management. 19 years old, he started playing tennis at the age of 4 and still plays to this day. His strongest shots are Forehand and Serves.

Fun Fact Dada is fluent in multiple languages such as Arabic,English and French.



Aleksander Gaiarsa

Aleksander Gaiarsa (20 years old), a 3rd year student in Electrical Engineering with Management/Entrepreneurship option. He began playing tennis in his teens, developing a great passion and thus teaching the sport for the past 4yrs. Aleks is left handed, which grants him that deadly lefty serve; he also has great behind the line defense.



Jaime Rafael Cepeda Montufar

Jaime Rafael Cepeda Montufar was born on February 4th 1999 in Bogotá Colombia. He is currently in his 3rd year of BASc in Electrical Engineering. He began to play tennis at the age of 13. His best shot is the second first serve. He was born with an extreme case of clubfoot which needed surgery which led one of his legs to be 1 inch shorter than the other.



Renée Morin

Renée Morin is 25 years old and doing her PhD in performance and exercise physiology at the University of Ottawa. She started playing tennis when she was little, from playing alone against a wall and having her dad coach her growing up she is now very excited to be on the UOttawa tennis team and looking forward to another season with the team! Her “weapon” is her topspin forehand and being left handed gives her an extra edge against her opponents!



Nina Chen

Nina Chen is a 18 years old undergraduate student at the University of Ottawa. She started playing tennis at the age of 9 years old. Nina’s best shot is her forehand. Fun fact about her, she has an unhealthy obsession with cheese!



Alexandre Wilson

Alexandre Wilson is a 21-year-old tennis player from Oakville, Ontario who started playing tennis around the age of 8. At uOttawa, he studied History and Political Science, graduating as part of the class of 2020. His best shot is his crosscourt backhand and a fun fact about him is that his favourite athlete growing up was former Montréal Canadiens captain Saku Koivu.



Andrea Parvan

Andrea Parvan is a 21 year old student at the University of Ottawa. She is currently in her fifth year in the Mathematics program. Andrea’s best shot is her forehand.




Max Zimmerman

Max Zimmerman is a graduate of the Honours program in Communication at uOttawa. He began playing tennis at the age of 6 and played competitively in Ontario as a junior, reaching a ranking of 8th in the province in 2007. His favourite shots are the backhand down the line and drop shot. Off the court you can usually find Max at the dog park with his Boston Terrier puppy named Macy.
