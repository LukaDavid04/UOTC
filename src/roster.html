Player Bios




Othman Ennaciri

(Men's Captain)

Born and raised in Morocco, Othman Ennaciri grew up in a tennis environment. From grand-father to grandson, the racket was handed to him at a very early age. He started competing at the age of 8 years old and was one of the top players in his country. Othman moved to Canada in 2014 at the age of 17, to pursue a Bachelor specialized in accounting at the University of Ottawa. He graduated in December 2018, and is now 23 years old. His best shot is his forehand.

Fun fact about Othman: At the age of 13, he forgot his shoes at home going to play a quarter final in a national tournament. He made sure that it doesn’t happen again.



Hannah Atkinson

(Women's Captain)

Hannah grew up playing competitive tennis in Toronto. She represented Team Ontario in the Junior Nationals from the age of 12 to 18. During her undergrad, Hannah played on the varsity Women’s Tennis Team at Williams College. She was a member of the 2015 NCAA National Championship Team. As a co-captain in 2017, she lead the team to win the NCAA National Team Championship. Also in 2017, she achieved an All-American status in doubles with partner Chloe Henderson. She received the Intercollegiate Tennis Association (ITA) National Most Improved Senior Award.

Fun Fact: While playing for the University of Ottawa Tennis Club, Hannah remains undefeated in singles and doubles.



Emma McShane

Born and raised in Halifax, Nova Scotia, Emma started playing tennis at the age of four and then went on to compete at the national level. In 2017, she was selected to be the captain of Team Nova Scotia at the Canada Summer Games in Winnipeg, Manitoba where she represented her province alongside her seven other teammates.

Tennis has always been an integral part of Emma’s life and she is very fortunate to be able to keep playing, even while pursuing her studies. Emma is currently an undergraduate student in Human Kinetics at the University of Ottawa.



Ryan Zanganeh

Ryan Zanganeh has been playing tennis since the age of 6. Most Valuable Player (MVP) of his high school tennis team, Ryan has one of the strongest two-handed backhands on the UOTC. He has trained and competed internationally, including at the Mouratoglou Tennis Academy; one of the best tennis academies in the world. Idolizing Roger Federer, he is best known for his passion towards the sport, fighter mentality, and aggressive play style on court. Ryan’s teammates feel his personality and style of play on court best resemble one of Italy’s best known tennis players; Fabio Fognini.




Sara Zeineddine

Sara Zeineddine is a 3rd year Political Science student. She started playing tennis at the age 7 & her love for tennis kept growing every year since then. Serena Williams is one of her biggest inspirations.

Sara has been competing since the age of 9, she mostly competed in ITF tournaments & national tournaments in Lebanon, DRC & Spain.. She is known to be very aggressive on court, but very friendly outside the court. Her teammates often compare her to Bianca Andreescu. She is very happy to be a part of the team & grateful to be able to play tennis while studying at the university. Fun fact, Sara loves to cook for family & friends, especially lebanese dishes.



Luka David

Luka began playing tennis in Whitby, Ontario at the age of 13. Before high school, he moved to Windsor, Ontario and began to compete and practice in the United States. He achieved a top 20 ranking in South-East Michigan whilst competing in statewide tournaments in from Michigan, Ohio and Indiana. He represented his school at OFSSA and won the MVP award for his team. He enjoyed playing in Serbia over the summers, and making trips to South Carolina for tennis camps. His favourite player is Novak Djokovic, whom he met at the Western and Southern Open.

Fun Fact: He was given the nickname Wawrinka because of his one-handed backhand.
