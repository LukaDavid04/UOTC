Why Join the UOTC?


The UOTC is a bilingual club structured for any student interested in learning and/or playing tennis. Tennis is an incredibly fun sport to learn and play. The physical and mental benefits of participating in any type of physical activity or exercise are extremely beneficial to improving your overall health and well-being. We highly recommend you consider joining our club. The UOTC has Tennis Canada certified coaches that can help you improve your game or introduce you to a healthier lifestyle.


What’s the Difference Between the Three Divisions?


The UOTC is composed of three divisions tailored to different players’ tennis levels; Division 1, Division 2, and Division 3. Division 1 is for students who are competitive and experienced tennis players seeking to train and compete regularly throughout each semester. Division 2 is for students who consider themselves intermediate tennis players; students who play the sport, but are less experienced, and are interested in practicing. Division 3 is for students with little or no background in tennis. This division is specific for passionate, and curious students who want to learn the basics in how to play tennis.


How Much Does It Cost to Join the UOTC?


After tryouts, our coaching staff will decide which division best suits your level of tennis. Once you join the division, there is a membership fee to pay. The membership fee for each division is determined by the Treasurer. The price may vary each depending on courts fees, the numbers of weekly practices, and the numbers of players in your division. Players will be contacted regarding their membership fee after the division rosters are determined. All players of the same division must pay the same fee; regardless of their level of tennis and/or whether they are a new or returning UOTC player.


How Many Practices are there for Each Division?


Division 1 has three weekly practices, each of which is two hours long. Division 2 and 3 have two weekly practices, each of which are one to one and a half hours long.


Where are Practices Held?


UOTC holds all practices at the Rideau Sports Center (RSC).


Do I Have to Attend Every Single Practice?


If you are in Division 1, please refer to the Division 1 Player Commitment Policy. If you are in Division 2 or Division 3, practices are NOT mandatory.


I Missed Tryouts but I am still Interested in Joining. What do I do?


Please feel free to contact us.


I Can No Longer Commit to the UOTC. Can I get a Refund of my Membership Fee?


Yes and no. Upon request, any player who decides they can no longer play on the UOTC can receive a full refund within THREE weeks of having paid their membership fee. However, AFTER the three weeks have expired, there is no guarantee for a full refund.


Do You Provide Tennis Rackets for Players?


Usually, players are required to bring a racket. However, if you do not have one, please contact us in advance and we will make arrangements to get you one.


I’m Looking to Buy Tennis Gear. Where is the Best Place in Ottawa to do so?


Looking for the best new tennis equipment in Ottawa? Performance Tennis Shop run by our good friend Gary Hawkes is the place to go.


I Want to Donate to the UOTC. What do I do?


Please feel free to contact us directly.


Who Runs the UOTC?


The UOTC is run by the five executive members: President, Treasurer, Marketing Executive, Communications Executive, and our Website Developer Executive. For more information about who they are, please refer to our Executives page. For more information on their specific roles towards the UOTC, please read our club’s constitution.
