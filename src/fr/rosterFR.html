Player Bios




Othman Ennaciri

(Capitaine des hommes)

Né et a grandi au Maroc, Othman Ennaciri a grandi dans un environnement de tennis. De grand-père à petit-fils, la raquette lui a été remise très tôt. Il a commencé la compétition à l'âge de 8 ans et était l'un des meilleurs joueurs de son pays. Othman a déménagé au Canada en 2014 à l'âge de 17 ans pour poursuivre un baccalauréat spécialisé en comptabilité à l'Université d'Ottawa. Il a obtenu son diplôme en décembre 2018 et a maintenant 23 ans. Son meilleur coup est son coup droit.

Un fait amusant à propos d’Othman: à l'âge de 13 ans, il a oublié ses chaussures à la maison en allant jouer un quart de finale dans un tournoi national. Il a fait en sorte que cela ne se reproduise plus.



Hannah Atkinson

(Women's Captain)

Hannah grew up playing competitive tennis in Toronto. She represented Team Ontario in the Junior Nationals from the age of 12 to 18. During her undergrad, Hannah played on the varsity Women’s Tennis Team at Williams College. She was a member of the 2015 NCAA National Championship Team. As a co-captain in 2017, she lead the team to win the NCAA National Team Championship. Also in 2017, she achieved an All-American status in doubles with partner Chloe Henderson. She received the Intercollegiate Tennis Association (ITA) National Most Improved Senior Award.

Fun Fact: While playing for the University of Ottawa Tennis Club, Hannah remains undefeated in singles and doubles.



Emma McShane

Née et a grandi à Halifax, Nouvelle-Écosse, Emma a commencé a joué au tennis dès l’âge de 4 ans et a ensuite concouru au niveau national. En 2017, elle a été sélectionnée pour le rôle de capitaine dans l’équipe de la Nouvelle-Écosse durant les jeux d’été du Canada à Winnipeg, Manitoba où elle a représenté sa province avec ses sept autres coéquipières.

Le tennis a toujours été une partie intégral de la vie d’Emma et elle est très chanceuse de pouvoir jouer tout en continuant ses études. Emma est une étudiante de premier cycle dans le programme d’Activité Physique de l’Université d’Ottawa.



Ryan Zanganeh

Ryan has been playing tennis since the age of 6. Most Valuable Player (MVP) of his high school tennis team, Ryan has one of the strongest two-handed backhands on the UOTC. He has trained and competed internationally, including at the Mouratoglou Tennis Academy; one of the best tennis academies in the world. Idolizing Roger Federer, he is best known for his passion towards the sport, fighter mentality, and aggressive play style on court. Ryan’s teammates feel his personality and style of play on court best resemble one of Italy’s best known tennis players; Fabio Fognini.




Sara Zeineddine

Sara is a 3rd year Political Science student. She started playing tennis at the age 7 & her love for tennis kept growing every year since then. Serena Williams is one of her biggest inspirations.

Sara has been competing since the age of 9, she mostly competed in ITF tournaments & national tournaments in Lebanon, DRC & Spain.. She is known to be very aggressive on court, but very friendly outside the court. Her teammates often compare her to Bianca Andreescu. She is very happy to be a part of the team & grateful to be able to play tennis while studying at the university. Fun fact, Sara loves to cook for family & friends, especially lebanese dishes.



Luka David

Luka began playing tennis in Whitby, Ontario at the age of 13. Before high school, he moved to Windsor, Ontario and began to compete and practice in the United States. He achieved a top 20 ranking in South-East Michigan whilst competing in statewide tournaments in from Michigan, Ohio and Indiana. He represented his school at OFSSA and won the MVP award for his team. He enjoyed playing in Serbia over the summers, and making trips to South Carolina for tennis camps. His favourite player is Novak Djokovic, whom he met at the Western and Southern Open.

Fun Fact: He was given the nickname Wawrinka because of his one-handed backhand.
